About Us

COVID-19 has had a devastating impact across the world, in terms of the economy, livelihoods, and of course, the health and well-being of people. Today, in addition to the reversal of some of our developmental gains, we are also faced with new challenges at an unprecedented scale â€“ increasing poverty, flagging economies, closing down of businesses, people losing their jobs, incomes and livelihoods, families losing members to COVID-19, children unable to attend school, children losing one or both of their parents to COVID-19, increasing instances of domestic and gender-based violence at home, de-prioritization of other developmental issues, among others.

While we are slowly making headway in tackling the virus, the battle to address the damage is ongoing and could take several years, if not decades, to overcome.

Now, more than ever, there is a greater need to unlock significant capital for innovations and initiatives that address these health and developmental concerns and need sustainable funding, especially where the government cannot invest, or there is lack of funding due to other conflicting priorities. Some of the best and innovative solutions to address these challenges come from smaller enterprises and organizations that work at the grassroots level, within affected and vulnerable communities. They have the vision to bring about positive change, but at times, lack the resources to do so.
